How Community Food Gardens Are Solving Land Access Barriers For SA Women And Youth - African Farming From rural villages to township neighbourhoods, local food-growing initiatives are becoming spaces where new agricultural knowledge is built, leadership develops and livelihoods begin to take shape.*. These emerging growers are increasingly visible through initiatives such as the **Shoprite Act For Change Food Garden Competition**, which recently recognised six community-based projects for their contribution to local food production and skills development. Beyond the vegetables harvested, the food gardens highlight how grassroots agricultural initiatives are helping women and youth participate more actively in South Africa’s food economy. More broadly, initiatives like the Act For Change competition highlight the growing role that grassroots agriculture plays in South Africa’s broader food system. They show that the future of agriculture will not only be shaped on large commercial farms, but also in neighbourhood plots where women, young people and communities are developing the skills, confidence and leadership needed to sustain food production for generations to come. https://www.africanfarming.com/2026/03/19/how-community-food-gardens-are-solving-land-access-barriers-for-sa-women-and-youth/
