Shropshire farmers 'hammered' by fertiliser and fuel rises - BBC A farmer has said his industry is struggling to keep produce affordable due to the rising price of fertiliser and red diesel. Andrew Williamson, who farms 900 acres of arable land near Bridgnorth in Shropshire, said he was concerned for next year's crop in the face of current "very expensive prices". Williamson said farms like his were already "struggling to break even and cover our cost of production" - but the rising price of fertiliser "makes that even more difficult". Since July 2025, when Williamson stocked up on fertiliser, the price had gone up by 50%, he said. Livestock farmers would be "hammered" by the price rise, Williamson added, as they bought fertiliser as and when they needed it, not in advance like arable farmers.
