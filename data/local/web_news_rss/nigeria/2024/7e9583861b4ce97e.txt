Reforms aim to enhance female farmers' status, expand economic activities, revise joint management system. The Ministry of Agriculture, Food and Rural Affairs announced on the 22nd that it has established the "6th Basic Plan for Fostering Female Farmers (2026–2030)," which includes measures to improve gender equality in rural society, such as transitioning the village leader election system from "one vote per household" to "one person, one vote." This plan, a mid- to long-term policy prepared every five years under the Act on the Promotion of Female Farmers, focuses on enhancing the status of female farmers, expanding their economic activities, and providing tailored welfare and health support in rural areas. The Ministry of Agriculture, Food and Rural Affairs will also revise the joint management system.
