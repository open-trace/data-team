All stacking up nicely: how brick manufacturer Forterra is evolving a centuries-old process - The Manufacturer Brickmaking dates back thousands of years, and at the Measham site of Forterra the soft-mud process still mirrors traditional methods—moulding clay and firing it in kilns—while being adapted for modern efficiency and scale. Forterra is diversifying beyond traditional bricks with products like the Omnia brick slip, which replicates the look of clay brick while reducing waste and supporting more efficient installation in modern construction projects. Around 60% of Forterra’s carbon footprint comes from the natural gas used in kilns, so the company is exploring solutions such as hydrogen-powered kilns, efficiency improvements and carbon capture to reduce emissions while maintaining production quality. Dave Manley, Forterra’s Head of Sustainability, explained: “Essentially, we’re trying to replicate what people did thousands of years ago, in terms of throwing clay into a mould and then firing it.”. The next stage of the trial process will be to convert one of Forterra’s smaller kilns to 100% hydrogen, which the company is aiming to have working this year. https://www.themanufacturer.com/articles/all-stacking-up-nicely-how-brick-manufacturer-forterra-is-evolving-a-centuries-old-process/
