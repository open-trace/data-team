What Community Gardens Mean For South Africa - African Farming *Across South Africa, community food gardens are quietly transforming local food systems. From township gardens and rural projects to school-based initiatives and youth-led farms, the finalists represent a growing network of small-scale agricultural projects that are helping communities grow their own food and improve food security. Projects such as the **Food Security Project in Gonubie**, where 162 women cultivate their own garden plots, and the **P Agricultural Group (PAG) in Soweto**, which distributes vegetables to more than 150 families each Christmas, demonstrate how community gardens can grow beyond subsistence and become hubs of food production and skills development. Together, these projects show that community gardens are not only producing food, but also building local agricultural capacity. While community gardens will never replace commercial agriculture, they play a crucial complementary role in South Africa’s food system. As initiatives like the Act for Change Food Garden Competition continue to highlight and support these projects, community agriculture is becoming an increasingly visible part of South Africa’s agricultural landscape. https://www.africanfarming.com/2026/03/22/what-community-gardens-mean-for-south-africa/
