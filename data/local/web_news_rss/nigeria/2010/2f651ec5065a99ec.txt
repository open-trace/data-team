Nebraska Farmer wins 2026 Outstanding Service Award for agricultural excellence - Farm Progress # Nebraska Farmer wins 2026 Outstanding Service Award for agricultural excellence. Left to right: Curt Arens, Nebraska Farmer senior editor; Elizabeth Hodges, Farm Progress staff writer; and Brad Lubben, Nebraska Extension policy specialist.Ryan Evans, UNL Ag Econ Department. (March 19, 2026) — The University of Nebraska-Lincoln (UNL) Department of Agricultural Economics has awarded Nebraska Farmer magazine, a Farm Progress publication, the prestigious 2026 Outstanding Service Award. The award recognizes Nebraska Farmer’s long-standing commitment to supporting Nebraska’s agricultural community. Nebraska Farmer’s collaboration with the Department of Agricultural Economics spans decades. Known as the world’s largest totally irrigated working farm show, Husker Harvest Days was founded in 1978 by Nebraska Farmer, in partnership with the Agricultural Institute of Nebraska and volunteers in Grand Island and Hall County. Husker Harvest Days, the world's largest totally irrigated working farm show, takes place annually in Grand Island, Nebraska. https://www.farmprogress.com/names-in-the-news/nebraska-farmer-wins-2026-outstanding-service-award-for-agricultural-excellence

The Ed Duren award is named for former hall of fame inductee and board member who, according to the hall of fame’s website, spent his 39-year career as a University of Idaho Extension Livestock Specialist, educator, author, and mentor to hundreds of young farmers and ranchers. Colby says it was Richard Larsen, a third-generation farmer from Rexburg and 2025 hall of fame inductee, who nominated him.
