Former Democratic lawmaker announces campaign for North Dakota agriculture commissioner - The Dakotan # Former Democratic lawmaker announces campaign for North Dakota agriculture commissioner. Former Democratic-NPL state lawmaker Vern Thompson announced Friday he’s running for North Dakota agriculture commissioner and has received endorsement from the party’s executive committee. “We need a voice to speak to that,” Thompson said in an interview with the North Dakota Monitor on Friday. Thompson represented the Minnewaukan area in the North Dakota House from 1989-1990 and served from 1997-2000 in the Senate. Initially, Thompson wanted to run for the Dem-NPL endorsement in North Dakota’s U.S. House race, but Dem-NPL delegates endorsed former U.S. House candidate Trygve Hammer to represent Democrats during the party’s state convention in Bismarck on March 7. Though party members did not select him for the federal race, Thompson said he still wanted to serve North Dakotans. Fargo, ND, USA / The Mighty 790 KFGO | KFGO. FARGO (KFGO) – After failing to receive Democratic party support to run for North Dakota’s Congressional seat, Fargo independent trucker Vern Thompson has announced that he will run for state Agriculture Commissioner in November. Thompson, making the announcement on KFGO News & Views, said he doesn’t believe long-time Republican Commissioner Doug Goehring is doing enough to support the state’s farmers.
