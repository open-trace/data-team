China spent $120 billion to lock down critical minerals overseas - Report - GhanaWeb China has invested more than $120 billion in overseas mining and upstream processing since 2023, accelerating a state-backed push to secure the raw materials underpinning the global energy transition, says Australian think tank Climate Energy Finance (CEF). The CEF research also finds that China’s outbound investment in mining is only one piece of a much larger industrial strategy. Chinese firms are increasingly partnering with host governments to build in-country processing facilities, infrastructure such as railways, ports and power systems, and local industrial capacity, in exchange for long-term supply agreements, says CEF founder Tim Buckley, who co-authored the report. By combining mining, processing and manufacturing, Beijing is strengthening its ability to influence pricing, availability and technology pathways in critical minerals markets, the CEF report notes, emphasizing that the trajectory remains one of expansion, not retreat, as China adapts its strategy to changing political and economic conditions.
